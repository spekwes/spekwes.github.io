Our Platform

A more detailed explanation of our viewpoints

College Tuition

  • Govt spent $91 billion on helping w/ college tuition in 2016 -> $37 billion went to tuition tax credits that disproportionately helped wealthier families. Relocate some of the funding to public colleges for tuition assistance instead.
  • Provide families below the 40th earning percentile of every state [only applicable to families earning under $75,000 yearly] with free public college opportunities.
  • Expand funding for work-study programs.

Gun Control

  • We advocate for the increase of resources dedicated to mental health and support. We will bring extensive background checks by extending the Federal Firearm License Ownership requirement.
  • We will make America a safer place to live as a whole by investigating various points of commerce that would usually be designated as private. This would allow us to live our lives without fear of mass gun-violence without infringing on anyone's constitutional rights.

Abortion

  • We want to provide people with the services they need anywhere in America without encroachment on the position of millions of citizens who don’t support it morally
  • We plan to construct abortion clinics funded by states that already have it legalized in states that don’t so that care can be readily accessed
  • We support abortion outside of the third trimester, but will allow exceptions if the life of the mother is endangered.

Social Media

  • The false advertisement of big social media platforms to the American public as platforms rather than publishers hurts American democracy.
  • Facebook’s Mark Zuckerberg told Congress in his April 2018 testimony that his company is “responsible for the content on its platform”. Besides him implying that Facebook is legally responsible for any non-licensed photos and verbal abuse on the network, saying that his network controls its content isn’t really consistent with what is legally a platform.
  • By definition, platform are companies that enable content and distribution of information. If social networks are a platform, then they cannot be liable for the material that is posted on their networks. You can’t sue them for defamation if someone publishes libelous content. They should just provide the platform, not the message.
  • It is fine if Facebook or any other large social media firm wants to be a publisher and hide certain kinds of information or media, but it must advertise itself as such. Having such large public outreach means that the curation of information can have an incredible impact on the political process in the United States

Affirmative Action

  • The current system is a negative feedback loop. Minority students that get accepted to certain colleges with less preparation for their new environment tend to fall behind very quickly and become increasingly lost as the instructors and the other students advance in the course. That of course damages their actual performance, solidifying the stereotype of minorities being inherently poor students.
  • It’s clear that colleges only use race-based affirmative action for positive public press. Once those minority students are a part of their diversity statistics, they don’t really care for them, rather only for lining their pockets with student debt once they drop out. (The current student debt total in the U.S. has exceeded $1.5 trillion, and millenials make up for a third of that)
  • By providing more opportunities for qualified applicants that might be economically challenged, there’s less of a chance for this to happen. Minority students would be able to enjoy college environments that put less pressure on them and allow them to do better.

Terrorism from the Middle East

  • We don’t want a massive policy change within the Middle East, and aim to maintain Teddy Roosevelt’s big stick policy.
  • We aim to support and condemn on a case by case basis:
    If a country helps us fight terrorism -> deliver American aid and support
    If a country commits acts of terrorism -> we apply pressure through means such as sanctions, and may escalate to military force if tensions escalate (ex: we would apply pressure to Saudi Arabia for the murder of journalist Jamal Khashoggi)

Immigration from Latin America

  • Increase number of family visas + the amount of refugees taken in each year.
  • Stronger background checks on all prospective immigrants
  • Streamline the process, and approve more L-1 visas
  • Reinstate DACA

China/Hong Kong Protests

  • As one of the fastest growing nations in the world, China is an extremely powerful trade nation. China currently accounts for $41 billion of trade per year. We believe that in keeping good relations with them, our economy will continue to prosper and the number of jobs will remain steady.
  • We still stay out of China and Hong Kong’s political business, as we are not in a position to do so. We cannot speak for the people of China and their desires and we do not feel entitled to decide for them without the participation of the international community. We will keep an eye on the situation, but find that military intervention would cause more harm than any benefits it could bring to the combined interests of Hong Kong and America.
