Joshua Spektor

Josh is a lifelong Coney Islander born to refugees from the former Soviet Union. His priority is to give every opportunity for future generations of Americans to succeed in this country, for he believes that the future of America is the backbone for its success. He is passionate about his platform and is a sharp, driven critical thinker. He is completing his fourth year as member of Stuyvesant’s math team, is the lead hockey writer for the Spectator newspaper, and is the cofounder of a custom clothing business. In his spare time, he enjoys reading, designing, playing ice hockey with friends, or worries about his CommonApp.




Lily Jin

Lily is a first generation Taiwanese American and is passionate about many issues that plague our country today. She has lived in Queens all her life in an immigrant community, and has had first-hand experience with many hardships people face. She is a self-driven, open, and free thinking person who will seek to make change. She is currently a Community Leader in the non-profit Direct Health Access, a research intern at NYU, and the Vice President of Stuyvesant Study Society. In her free time she likes to play music, cook, and watch too much Netflix.





Our Platform

  • Environment

    We support the battle against climate change, but feel a severe carbon tax would be detrimental to US industry.

  • College Tuition

    Everyone earning under a certain income has free tuition and others will have heavily reduced tuition.

  • Abortion

    We want to provide people with the services they need anywhere in America without encroaching upon the beliefs of those who do not morally support it.

  • Social Media

    The false advertisement of big social media platforms to the American public as platforms rather than publishers hurts American democracy.

  • Affirmative Action

    We advocate to replace race-based affirmative action with economic-based and merit-based aid.

  • Gun Control

    We support gun ownership and advocate for the increase of resources dedicated to mental health and support.

  • Terrorism From the Middle East

    We don’t want a massive policy change within the Middle East. We aim to maintain a position similar to Teddy Roosevelt’s big stick policy.

  • Immigration from Latin America

    We encourage skilled immigration by granting more family visas and cracking down on illegal immigration.

  • China/Hong Kong Protests

    We still stay out of China and Hong Kong’s political business as we are not in a position to interfere.
